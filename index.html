Welcome Nagahanons!

The journey awaits you

Barangay Cogon, located 17 kilometers north of the City of Naga, spans 715 hectares with 19 Puroks and a population of 4,267. It is home to Mt. Naupa, the city’s highest point, shared with Barangay Lanas. Nearby, Barangay Inoburan hosts a 28-hectare mangrove plantation with 17 species, ranking third in the Tourism Site Prioritization process.

The Dagitab Festival, the City of Naga's cultural celebration, begins on December 8 alongside the Feast of the Immaculada Concepcion and highlights its identity as a city of lights and progress. Known as a power hub with KEPCO and Therma Power Visayas in Barangay Colon, Naga also boasts vibrant public spaces like St. Francis of Assisi Ocean Park and the City of Naga Baywalk and Boardwalk Complex, a favorite spot for relaxation. The festival celebrates the city’s industrial growth, bringing hope and prosperity to all Nagahanons.
